Comparative Research

My Research

In my research I will be comparing two websites I know very well in posting stories that are shared to the public, and these stories can be a range from person experiences to other genres. Personal experiences or stories can tell a lot about a person and can create meaningful advice and creativity to share. We are all naturally drawn to reading something that sparks our interest and in return we enjoy it and take from it and learn from it and can connect us all.

Reddit: r/writing

Reddit is a forum posting site known to have many topics and sub topics, it can range from many catergories where people can post and get responses from other people on the site. Natually there is a thread dedicated to writing. And still thread people can post advice on writing, some common tips from character design, to placing a backstory at the right time, and the list goes on. What I find very interesting about Reddit is that the thread can be edited to look nice or to get the sense of theme on what this is about. It has a traditional forum like style so that you can find the post you want when upon search, and it filters through to the most recent post that is currently being commented. It can even display pinned posted of annoucements in this image which will stay on the first page as informational topics. The overall layout is simple and resembles a forum post which most people are familiar with and has a upper directory bar/nav bar, that can direct the user to another or more direct catergories.

Baka Tsuki: light novels

Baka Tsuki is a website of it's own that keeps up with updating light novels. Many go to this site whether you are the author of a very well know novel or show, or just someone starting out in writing, to post their work and get feedback but ultimately sharing some of the most creative pieces I have read. I myself am a big fan of light novels and how descriptive they are. The site is formally known to heavily influenced in Japanese culture such as manga and anime but essentially you would be getting the first content before other platforms. What I really like about the website layout is that it resembles Wikipedia or Wikia Fandom, again something many of us are familiar about. The layout is simple, the writings are put into catergories and in those catergories they are sorted alphabetical order making it easy to find the story you are looking for. The style is clean and not too distracting only to help you find the light novel you want to read and the side bar to the left gives information in relation to light novels and or updates to certain catergories. It's a very crisp clean site with little clicking to the next page.
